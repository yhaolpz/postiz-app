# Tiny Agent HyperFrames Motion Upgrade

## Style

Use the whiteboard motion system from `DESIGN.md`: board `#FCFDFC`, ink `#111413`, blue `#1597EA`, red `#F04438`, structural gray `#D9E1DE`; Montserrat for statements and IBM Plex Mono for captions and metadata.

## Rhythm

`question-SNAP -> simplify-BUILD -> evidence-HOLD`

## Global Rules

- Duration: `18.4s`, portrait `1080x1920`, `30fps`.
- Reuse the first three approved `Augmented LLM First` generated art layers and original narration.
- Keep title, caption, metadata, and progress layers fixed while each art layer receives a fixed-center slow push.
- Use a blue marker wipe at `5.3s` and `12.6s`; outgoing scenes remain complete until covered.
- Keep the main artwork in `y=300-1110`, captions in `y=1130-1430`, and only non-critical progress/metadata below.

## Scene 1 - Five Agents

- Timing: `0.0-5.5s`.
- Concept: Turn the crowded-agent meeting into a visual warning. The viewer should feel that complexity arrived before the problem was understood.
- Background: whiteboard surface, top registration rail, scene index, two short marker dashes.
- Midground: approved meeting keyframe, full-width hook, red `5x` warning stamp.
- Foreground: fixed caption frame, marker highlight under `FIVE`, progress rail, production metadata.
- Choreography: title snaps down; rule draws left-to-right; art scales from `0.94` and settles; warning stamp lands with restrained overshoot; caption switches once at the narration boundary.
- Transition: saturated-blue marker wipe, `0.6s`, left-to-right then retract from the right.

## Scene 2 - One Augmented Model

- Timing: `5.5-12.8s`.
- Concept: Replace the crowd with one capable center. Retrieval, tools, and memory become three clear capabilities rather than three more agents.
- Background: same registration rail and whiteboard structure for continuity.
- Midground: approved central Tiny Agent keyframe and three capability labels.
- Foreground: headline highlight, caption frame, scene index, active progress segment.
- Choreography: art rises into place; capability labels cascade from different directions; the blue headline marker sweeps after the words land; a subtle fixed-center push holds attention.
- Transition: repeat the marker wipe so the motion becomes a recognizable series grammar.

## Scene 3 - Retrieval Finds Facts

- Timing: `12.8-18.4s`.
- Concept: Make retrieval tangible by focusing attention on the selected fact instead of the discarded pile. The magnifying glass is the hero, not another diagram.
- Background: persistent rails plus a short blue focus line.
- Midground: approved retrieval keyframe and an `OUTSIDE FACT` locator tag.
- Foreground: caption frame, final scene index, completed progress rail.
- Choreography: title enters from the left; art settles from a slight scale reduction; locator tag clicks into place; underline draws under `RIGHT FACT`; the frame holds through the final spoken word.

## Recurring Motifs

- Blue marker sweeps indicate the correct idea.
- Red stamps indicate premature complexity.
- Scene indices and progress rail make the explanation feel intentionally edited.
- Every art push uses the same optical center discipline as the current renderer.

## Negative Prompt

No new character drawings, no dark tech aesthetic, no gradient, no glass cards, no dashboard grid, no tiny labels, no full-frame zoom, no subtitles below `y=1430`, no jump cuts, and no animation that changes the approved artwork itself.
