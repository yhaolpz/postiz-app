Most people still use AI like a search box with a better writing style. They ask one question, read one answer, and do the real work themselves.

An agent changes the unit of work. Instead of asking for advice, you delegate a longer task that may inspect files, use tools, make changes, check results, and keep going until it reaches a defined outcome.

OpenAI's 2026 Codex research shows the shift. In May, more than seventy percent of sampled users made at least one request estimated above an hour of human work, and one quarter made one above eight hours. But longer does not automatically mean suitable for delegation.

You will learn how to recognize an agent-ready task, place context and review around it, and choose among chat, copilot, supervised agent, and delegated workflow.

You will leave with a Task Upgrade Scorecard for research, documents, data, publishing, operations, and software work.
