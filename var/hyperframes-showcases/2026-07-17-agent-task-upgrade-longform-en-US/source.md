# Source

- Publisher: OpenAI
- Title: How agents are transforming work
- Published: 2026-06-25
- Canonical URL: https://openai.com/index/how-agents-are-transforming-work/
- Research paper: The Shift to Agentic AI: Evidence from Codex

## Confirmed source claims

- Agentic AI changes the unit of work from a short interaction to a delegated, longer-horizon task that may use tools, inspect environments, and iterate toward a result.
- In May 2026, 80.6% of sampled individual Codex users made at least one request estimated to exceed 30 minutes of human work, 70.2% exceeded one hour, and 25.6% exceeded eight hours.
- Non-developer adoption grew quickly, and usage extended into documents, data analysis, communication, research, product, recruiting, sales, and other knowledge work.
- Intensive users were more likely to run longer, repeatable, and parallel workflows and to use skills that preserve procedural context.
- Delegation creates new requirements for supervision, verification, coordination, permissions, and workflow redesign.

## Fact boundaries

- Human-time estimates come from an automated classifier and are directional rather than exact.
- The OpenAI employee population is an unusually favorable adoption environment and is not representative of a typical organization today.
- The Task Upgrade Scorecard in this video is a practical synthesis from the source evidence. It is not presented as an original table from OpenAI.
