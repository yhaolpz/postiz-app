Decision-ready research does not begin with a broad topic and end with a pile of links. It begins with one accountable decision and a plan for turning evidence into a usable next action.

First, define the audience, decision, deliverable, scope, and appropriate research depth.

Second, plan sub-questions and sources, then require citations, quality checks, and visible conflicts.

Third, expose missing evidence and confidence, and deliver the recommendation with its limits and next action.

Use the Decision-Ready Research Brief on your next investigation. If the decision, scope, source strategy, evidence standard, gaps, or deliverable is vague, repair that field before asking for more depth.
