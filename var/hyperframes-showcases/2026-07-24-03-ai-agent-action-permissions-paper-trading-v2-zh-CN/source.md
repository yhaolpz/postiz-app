## Confirmed source claims

- Prompt injection can place instructions inside external content and induce an Agent to take actions the user did not request.
- Defenses should constrain what manipulated information can reach, especially consequential tools and sensitive-data transmissions.
- High-risk actions should expose the proposed consequence to the user and require confirmation, or be blocked when the request violates deterministic limits.
- The permission model in this video is an editorial teaching tool derived from the source-and-sink framing, confirmation behavior, blocking behavior, and deterministic capability limits. It is not presented as an OpenAI-named framework.

## Story adaptation

The video uses one computer-office scenario: a person running a one-person business gives an Agent access to public investment research and a simulated account. Research, local calculations, drafts, simulation, and replay remain automatic. Real orders, transfers, outbound communication, or permission changes require explicit human confirmation. Credential requests, unknown recipients, disabled risk limits, and hidden external instructions are blocked.
