Good agent work does not begin with a blank prompt. It begins with real materials and a concrete artifact that another person can inspect, edit, and continue.

First, define the artifact and provide the smallest complete source package.

Second, make constraints, review expectations, and destination format observable.

Third, return a review-ready first version with evidence, open questions, and a clear handoff.

Use the Agent Task Brief on your next assignment. If the artifact, sources, constraints, review, format, or handoff is vague, repair that field before giving the agent more tools or authority.
