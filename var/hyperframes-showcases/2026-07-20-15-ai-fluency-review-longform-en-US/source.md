## Confirmed findings

- The study analyzed 9,830 substantive, multi-turn Claude.ai conversations and measured 11 of the 24 behaviors in the 4D AI Fluency Framework that were observable inside chat.
- 85.7% of sampled conversations showed iteration and refinement. Those conversations displayed 2.67 additional fluency behaviors on average, versus 1.33 in non-iterative conversations.
- Conversations with iteration were 5.6 times more likely to question Claude's reasoning and 4 times more likely to identify missing context.
- Artifact-producing conversations represented 12.3% of the sample. They showed more goal clarification, format specification, examples, and iteration, but lower rates of identifying missing context, checking facts, and questioning reasoning.
- Only 30% of conversations included explicit instructions about how Claude should interact with the user.

## Boundaries

- The results are correlational and do not establish that iteration causes other fluency behaviors.
- The sample covers Claude.ai multi-turn users in a single week and is not a universal benchmark for all people, tasks, or AI systems.
- Only 11 observable behaviors were measured; evaluation may occur through testing, external tools, colleagues, or private judgment outside the chat.
- Binary behavior labels do not capture quality, depth, or partial demonstrations.
