Now turn the framework into a 4D AI Fluency Card. Score the current task, not your identity. The purpose is to find the weak link before you give AI more context, tools, or authority.

Use a simple scale: zero means the decision is missing, one means it is partly explicit, and two means it is clear and testable. Do not hide a zero inside a high total. Any missing dimension can block the workflow.

For Delegation, ask: Is the desired outcome specific? Have I chosen what AI does and what remains human? Are tools, permissions, stop conditions, and handoff evidence bounded? If not, keep the task in collaboration.

For Description, ask: Does the brief include relevant context, audience, constraints, examples only where useful, and an acceptance test? Could another session act on it without guessing what “good” means?

For Discernment, ask: Have I checked task fit, important claims, assumptions, and uncertainty? Did I compare evidence or test a failure case where the result matters? Polished language earns no automatic point.

For Diligence, ask: Is the action reversible, authorized, recorded, and verified afterward? Do I know who owns the final decision? Before consequential execution, require two points in every dimension, not merely a good average.

First, score the task from zero to two on each of the four dimensions.

Second, treat any zero as a reason to stop and repair the workflow.

Third, expand AI authority only after all four dimensions are testable.
